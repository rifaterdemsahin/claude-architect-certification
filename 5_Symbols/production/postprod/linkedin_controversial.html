🔥 Audience Growth Playbook

Controversial Post Playbook

How to create image-based LinkedIn posts that spark debate, attract the AI transformation audience, and turn passive scrollers into followers.

Why controversy works
🔥

Comment bait

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards comments 4× more than likes. A bold claim forces people to agree or defend themselves.

🧠

Cognitive friction

The brain stops scrolling when a statement contradicts a held belief. That pause is your window.

📢

Signal amplification

Disagreement spreads posts to the commenter's network — your audience grows via people who argue with you.

🎯

Audience filtering

Only people who care about AI transformation will engage. Low-quality followers self-select out.

Anatomy of an image-based controversial post

5-layer structure

📐 Post Structure — top to bottom
1
🖼️ Hook image — the scroll-stopper Bold white text on dark gradient. One provocative claim, max 10 words. No logo spam, no stock photos. The image must work without a caption. Use Canva or Figma: 1080×1080px, high contrast, large type (>60pt).
2
🪝 Opening line — the first 2 sentences (shown before "see more") Restate the claim as a direct statement. Add one surprising stat or personal stake. Never start with "I". Example: "Most AI pilots fail before month 3. Not because of the model — because of the org."
3
📖 Body — 3-5 short punchy paragraphs Each paragraph = 1-2 sentences max. Use line breaks aggressively. No walls of text. List the reasons, the evidence, or the story that backs the claim. Alternate between facts and opinion to keep both sides engaged.
4
💬 Engagement trigger — the question or dare End with a direct question that forces a side: "Agree or disagree?" / "What's your org's failure point?" / "Drop your take below." Never ask open-ended vague questions — binary or specific only.
5
#️⃣ Hashtags — 3-5 max, targeted #AITransformation #EnterpriseAI #Claude #LLM #ArchitectLife — always include at least one niche tag (low volume, high intent) alongside 1-2 broad tags. Never more than 5 or it looks spammy.

Creating the hook image

🎨 Step-by-step: build the image in Canva (5 min)

  1. Open Canva → New Design → Custom size: 1080 × 1080 px
  2. Background: Use a dark gradient — deep purple (#0f0c29 → #24243e) or dark navy (#0a0e27 → #1a1a2e). Avoid white or light backgrounds; they blend into the feed.
  3. Text: Add your claim in large sans-serif bold (Outfit, Montserrat, or Inter). Font size 80–100pt. White (#FFFFFF) or bright orange (#fb923c) for the trigger word. Left-aligned or centred — never justified.
  4. One accent element only: A thin horizontal line, a small arrow, or an emoji. Nothing more — clutter kills the message.
  5. Watermark: Your name or domain in 12pt grey (#6b7280) bottom-right. Small enough to not distract.
  6. Export: PNG, 1080×1080. No JPEG compression artefacts on text.

💡 Speed tip: Copy the image text prompt below into Midjourney or fal.ai with the style "minimalist dark corporate LinkedIn post, bold white typography, no people, gradient background" for an AI-generated version.

Before you hit publish

✅ Pre-post checklist

  • Image makes a clear, debatable claim in under 10 words
  • First 2 lines of text work without the image context
  • Post has exactly one question at the end (not two or three)
  • No more than 5 hashtags
  • You can defend the claim in the comments — you'll need to
  • Post goes live on Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am or 12–1pm (audience timezone)
  • Reply to every comment within the first 60 minutes to feed the algorithm
  • Do NOT delete or edit after posting — edits reset distribution
