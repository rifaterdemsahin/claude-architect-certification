🔍 Customer Discovery

Macro Foundation → Micro Execution

Based on the two frameworks, the ideal logical order flows from the macro-philosophical foundation (the pyramid) to the micro-execution steps (the workflow chart).

Part 1: The Foundational Mindset (The Pyramid)

Part 1: The Foundational Mindset (The Pyramid)

Before executing any steps, you must understand why you are doing them. You build from the bottom up:

✅
1. 🕵️ Customer Discovery Going out to talk to real people to validate assumptions.
✅
2. 🩹 Solving a Problem or Pain Point Using those insights to address a genuine friction point.
✅
3. 💎 Providing Value to Users Turning that solution into something so useful that customers care about it.
✅
4. 🏆 Successful Business The ultimate peak achieved only after the bottom three layers are solid.

Part 2: The Step-by-Step Customer Discovery Process

Part 2: The Step-by-Step Customer Discovery Process

This is the chronological execution path based on Steve Blank’s Customer Development methodology.

📝

Phase 1: Formulate Hypotheses (Write down your assumptions)

✅
1. Product Hypothesis

What are you building, and what features do you think it needs?

✅
2. Customer & Problem Hypothesis

Who do you think has this problem, and how severe is it?

✅
3. Distribution & Pricing Hypothesis

How will you reach these customers, and how much will they pay?

✅
4. Demand Creation Hypothesis

How will you acquire and grow your user base?

✅
5. Market Type Hypothesis

Are you entering an existing market, cloning one, or creating a brand new one?

✅
6. Competitive Hypothesis

Who are the current competitors, and how will you beat them?

🗣️

Phase 2: Test the Problem Hypothesis (Talk to people)

✅
7. First Contacts

Reach out to potential customers who fit your target persona.

✅
8. Problem Presentation

Share the problem (not your solution yet!) to see if it resonates with them.

✅
9. Customer Understanding

Listen deeply to how they currently cope with this issue.

✅
10. Market Knowledge

Use these conversations to build a broader understanding of the market landscape.

💡

Phase 3: Test the Product Hypothesis (Show the solution)

✅
11. First Reality Check

Take your initial feedback and align it with your product concept.

✅
12. Product Presentation

Show a mockup, prototype, or MVP (Minimum Viable Product) to the customers.

✅
13. More Customer Visits

Gather deep feedback from a wider pool of users on the prototype.

✅
14. Second Reality Check

Analyze whether the product actually solves the problem well enough for them to buy it.

⚖️

Phase 4: Verify and Decide (The Moment of Truth)

✅
15. Verify the Product

Does the product actually work for the user?

✅
16. Verify the Problem

Is this a must-solve problem that customers are willing to pay for?

✅
17. Verify the Business Model

Can you make money, scale, and acquire customers sustainably?

✅
18. Iterate or Exit

Based on the data, either pivot, proceed, or stop.
